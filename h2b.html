What is the H-2B Visa?

The H-2B visa program allows U.S. employers to hire foreign workers for temporary non-agricultural jobs when there are not enough U.S. workers available. This program ensures that businesses can meet their labor needs during peak periods.

Benefits of the H-2B Program

Participating in the H-2B program offers several advantages:

  • Access to a Reliable Workforce: Ensure your operations run smoothly during peak seasons by filling labor shortages with qualified workers.
  • Compliance with Labor Laws: The program provides a legal pathway to employ foreign workers, helping you stay compliant with federal regulations.
  • Flexibility: Hire workers for various types of temporary needs, including seasonal, peak load, and one-time occurrences.

Employer Responsibilities

Employers participating in the H-2B program must adhere to specific requirements:

  • Job Requirements: The work must be temporary, and employers must prove that there are not enough U.S. workers available.
  • Wages: Employers must pay H-2B workers the highest of the prevailing wage, federal minimum wage, or state minimum wage.
  • Worker Protections: Employers must ensure that they do not displace U.S. workers and must comply with wage and hour regulations.
