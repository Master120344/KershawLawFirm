H-2B Visa Program

The H-2B Visa Program enables U.S. employers to hire foreign workers for temporary, non-agricultural jobs when there’s a shortage of U.S. workers. Administered by the U.S. Department of Labor (DOL) and U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), it supports industries like hospitality, landscaping, and construction during peak seasons.

Key Features

  • Purpose: Fills seasonal, peak-load, or one-time non-agricultural roles (e.g., resort staff, carnival workers).
  • Duration: Up to 10 months initially, extendable to 3 years max, with a 60-day departure requirement.
  • Cap: Limited to 66,000 visas annually (33,000 per half-year), often exhausted quickly.
  • Eligibility: Workers from designated countries (e.g., Mexico, Philippines, Haiti as of 2024).

Employer Responsibilities

Employers must comply with stringent regulations:

  • Demonstrate a temporary need and no available U.S. workers through recruitment.
  • Pay the prevailing wage as determined by DOL for the job and location.
  • Provide return transportation if the worker completes the job or is dismissed early.
  • Ensure working conditions don’t adversely affect U.S. workers.

Recent Updates (2025)

As of January 17, 2025, DHS introduced reforms to strengthen the program:

  • Enhanced penalties for employers charging illegal fees, risking 1-3 year bans.
  • Cross-program debarment for labor violations in H-2A or H-2B.
  • FY 2025 supplemental allocation: 64,716 additional visas (44,716 for returning workers).
