H-2A Visa Program

The H-2A Visa Program allows U.S. agricultural employers to hire foreign workers for temporary or seasonal jobs when there are insufficient U.S. workers available. Managed by the U.S. Department of Labor (DOL) and U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), it supports farmers facing labor shortages while ensuring fair treatment for workers.

Key Features

  • Purpose: Fills temporary agricultural roles (e.g., planting, harvesting, livestock care).
  • Duration: Typically up to 10 months, extendable to 3 years max, followed by a 60-day departure period.
  • No Cap: Unlike H-2B, thereâ€™s no annual limit on H-2A visas.
  • Eligibility: Workers from designated countries (e.g., Mexico, Jamaica, Belize as of 2024).

Employer Responsibilities

Employers must meet strict requirements to participate:

  • Prove no U.S. workers are available via recruitment efforts.
  • Pay the highest of the Adverse Effect Wage Rate (AEWR), prevailing wage, or minimum wage.
  • Provide free housing meeting OSHA standards and transportation (inbound/outbound and worksite).
  • Guarantee 75% of work hours in the contract period (the "3/4 Guarantee").

Recent Updates (2025)

As of January 17, 2025, new DHS regulations enhance worker protections:

  • Penalties for prohibited fees (e.g., recruitment costs) can lead to 1-3 year petition denials.
  • Denials for serious labor violations across H-2A/H-2B programs.
  • Belize added to eligible countries (Nov 2024).
