Frequently Asked Questions

Your H-2A and H-2B visa questions, answered with expertise.

What are H-2A and H-2B visas?

The H-2A visa is for temporary agricultural workers, while the H-2B visa is for non-agricultural temporary workers. Both allow U.S. employers to hire foreign nationals when there are insufficient U.S. workers available, subject to strict labor certifications.

Who qualifies for an H-2A visa?

Employers in agriculture (e.g., farms, nurseries) needing seasonal or temporary workers qualify. You must prove a shortage of U.S. workers and that hiring foreigners won’t harm local wages or conditions.

What industries use H-2B visas?

H-2B visas are common in hospitality, landscaping, construction, and tourism. They’re capped annually at 66,000, so timing is critical. We’ll guide you through the process seamlessly.

How long does the visa process take?

Typically, 60-90 days, depending on labor certification, USCIS processing, and consular interviews. Our prescreening ensures your application is optimized for speed and approval.

What costs should I expect?

Costs include filing fees ($460-$1,000+), labor certification expenses, and worker transportation. We provide transparent estimates tailored to your needs during prescreening.

Can workers extend their stay?

Yes, H-2A/H-2B visas can be extended in one-year increments, up to three years total, if the need persists. We handle extensions with precision to keep your workforce intact.
